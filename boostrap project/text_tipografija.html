Display Headings


Display headings in the field of IT serve as attention-grabbing elements, featuring larger font sizes and lighter font weights compared to standard headings.

They are often employed to highlight key sections or titles on websites and applications, making it easier for users to navigate and find relevant information swiftly.

Whether in web development, user interfaces, or content design, display headings play a crucial role in enhancing the visual hierarchy and user experience.

Lighter, Secondary Text


In various applications within the IT sphere, the small HTML element is frequently harnessed to generate lighter, secondary text in headings.

This subtler, understated text often serves to provide additional information, explanations, or footnotes while maintaining a clear hierarchy within the content.

It contributes to a more streamlined user experience, ensuring that users can easily differentiate between primary and secondary information on websites, applications, or documents.

This practice of employing the small element for lighter, secondary text in headings is a key typographical technique used to enhance readability and effectively convey information across various IT platforms and digital media.

The small element, when judiciously utilized in typographic compositions, plays a crucial role in enhancing the user experience and conveying intricate IT-related information with clarity and precision.
